    Coulomb friction

    In the case of Coulomb friction there are two general cases for the friction force \( f \) \[f = \begin{cases} \dot{q} = 0 : |f| < μmg \\ \dot{q} \neq 0 : |f| = μmg \end{cases}\] where \( mg \) is always the magnitude of the normal force and \( μ \) the coefficient of friction. This can be written in the very compact condition \[0 ≤ μ mg - |f| ⟂ \dot{q}\] By introducing slack variables \( f^+ \) , \( f^- \) where \( f = f^+ - f^- \) and \( v_\text{abs} = |\dot{q}| \) we may convert the condition into an LCP \[\operatornamewithlimits{find}_{v_\text{abs},f^+,f^-} \ \text{s.t.}: \begin{aligned} \quad 0 &\leq v_\text{abs} + v &\perp& & f^+ &≥ 0 \\ \quad 0 &\leq v_\text{abs} - v &\perp& & f^- &≥ 0 \\ \quad 0 &\leq μmg - f^+ -f^- &\perp& & v_\text{abs} &≥ 0 \end{aligned}\]

    source: Example B.5 of Russ Tedrake’s ‘Underactuated’ notes
